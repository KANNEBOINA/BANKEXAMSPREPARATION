Six Countries Enter UN Security Council
i. Equatorial Guinea, Ivory Coast, Kuwait, Peru, Poland and the Netherlands 
formally joined the ranks of the non-permanent members of the United Nations 
Security Council to make a difference.
ii. The UN Security Council has 15 members, including five with permanent seats who have the power to veto resolutions - Britain, China, France, Russia and the United States. The six countries who left the 
UN Security Council recently are Egypt, Italy, Japan, Senegal, Ukraine and Uruguay.

The UN Security Council held its first session on 17 January 1946 in London.
